Employee Increment Policy Based on Performance
Introduction
At ABC Technologies, salary increments are determined based on performance evaluations to ensure fairness, motivation, and recognition for exceptional contributions. The increment percentage is based on employees’ Total Weighted Score, ensuring that high-performing individuals are rewarded accordingly.

The Min Raw Score is the lowest possible sum if an employee gets the lowest ratings in each KPI.
The Max Raw Score is the highest possible sum if an employee gets the highest ratings in each KPI.
Min Raw Score
(1 × 30%) + (1 × 25%) + (1 × 20%) + (1 × 15%) + (1 × 10%) = 1.00
Max Raw Score
(5 × 30%) + (5 × 25%) + (5 × 20%) + (5 × 15%) + (5 × 10%) = 5.00
Step 3: Apply the Normalization Formula
Purpose of Normalization:
The Total Weighted Score is calculated from multiple performance KPIs, each weighted differently. However, the raw score could exceed 5.00 or drop below 3.00, which doesn’t fit within the designed salary increment ranges.

Normalization ensures that the final Weighted Score is scaled to always fall between 3.00 and 5.00, making it compatible with the increment calculation.




Score Normalization
To scale the Raw Score into the desired range (3.00 to 5.00), use following formula:
Normalized Score = 3.00 + ((Raw Score – Min. Raw Score) ÷ (Max. Raw Score – Min. Raw Score)) × 2.00
Using our example: Final Score = 3.00 + ((4.455 - 1.00)/4.00) × 2.00 = 4.73
Breaking it down
(Raw Score – Min. Raw Score) ÷ (Max. Raw Score – Min. Raw Score) converts the raw score into a proportion between 0 and 1.
Multiplying by 2.00 spreads the score across the 3.00 to 5.00 range.
Adding 3.00 ensures the lowest value starts from 3.00, not 0.

Formula for Increment Calculation
The salary increment percentage depends on the employee’s weighted score, using the following structure:
Outstanding Performance (4.50 - 5.00) → 20% - 25%
Employees in this range start at 20% and increase up to 25% as their score approaches 5.00.
The closer they are to 5.00, the higher the increment they receive.
Formula:
Increment = 20 + (Weighted Score - 4.50) × (5 ÷ 0.50)
Exceeds Expectations (4.00 - 4.49) → 15% - 19.99%
Employees scoring exactly 4.00 receive a 15% increment.
As the score moves closer to 4.49, the percentage gradually increases up to 19.99%.
Formula:
Increment = 15 + (Weighted Score - 4.00) × (4.99 ÷ 0.49)

Meets Expectations (3.50 - 3.99) → 10% - 14.99%
A score of 3.50 gets a 10% increment.
As the score approaches 3.99, the increment grows up to 14.99%.
Formula:
Increment = 10 + (Weighted Score - 3.50) × (4.99 ÷ 0.49)

Below Expectations, With Potential (3.00 - 3.49) → 5% - 8%
Employees with a 3.00 score receive the minimum 5% increment.
Higher scores in this range bring slightly better increments up to 8%.
Formula:
Increment = 5 + (Weighted Score - 3.00) × (3 ÷ 0.49)
Needs Improvement (Below 3.00) → 0%
No salary increment is given for scores below 3.00.
Employees in this range are encouraged to focus on improvement plans before qualifying for an increase.
